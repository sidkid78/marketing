The Imperative of Aging-in-Place:
A Growing Market Opportunity

[Visual 2: Demographic Shift & Spend]

This document explores how HOMEase AI is set to become the essential bridge connecting a vast, underserved market with highly qualified professionals, particularly by strategically aligning with critical federal funding initiatives like the HUD Older Adults Home Modification Program.

The demand for Aging-in-Place solutions is not merely a trend but a powerful demographic imperative. Driven by an aging global population, the number of U.S. adults aged 60 and over surged by 33% between 2010 and 2020. Crucially, a significant majority—often cited between 75% and 87% of adults aged 50 and older—express a profound desire to remain in their current homes as they age.

The Accessibility Gap

This strong consumer preference collides with a stark reality: a vast accessibility gap where only 3.5% to 10% of U.S. homes meet basic accessibility standards, and a staggering 90% lack the necessary modifications for safe aging.

By 2035, projections indicate that 17 million older households will include at least one person with a mobility disability, representing a 77% increase from current figures. This confluence of factors creates a sustained, growing demand for modification services, propelling the total addressable market (TAM) for AIP solutions from an estimated $105.8 billion in 2024 to a projected $161.0 billion by 2030. Industry activity underscores this trend, with 76% of remodelers reporting an increase in aging-in-place project requests between 2020 and 2023. Common modifications range from simple grab bar installations costing $90-$300, to more extensive projects like walk-in showers ($1,500-$15,000) or stairlift installations ($4,000-$8,000), highlighting the diverse and substantial nature of this market.

HOMEase AI: Technology at the Forefront

At its core, HOMEase AI is a capital-efficient, highly scalable pure-play lead generation platform designed to address critical inefficiencies in the home modification process. Operating on a pay-per-lead model, contractors access qualified project opportunities, with initial pricing set at $40 per lead and a projected range of $50-$200, reflecting lead quality tiers, project complexity, and market demand.

The platform's value proposition is clear: it acts as a "project accelerator" for contractors by delivering leads significantly more likely to convert than those from general home service platforms, justifying a premium price. The primary competitive advantage of HOMEase AI lies in its proprietary Augmented Reality (AR) assessment tool.

[Visual 3: AR Dimensions & Analysis]
This innovative technology fundamentally transforms lead quality. Utilizing a device's camera (and LiDAR where available), the AR tool automatically detects architectural features, accurately measures dimensions, and identifies potential accessibility hazards and safety risks within a home. This process yields a highly validated lead by confirming explicit homeowner intent through active engagement, providing precise, data-backed requirements (e.g., specific ramp slopes, doorway widths), and capturing the homeowner's budget range and urgency level (Low, Medium, High). For contractors, this superior lead quality significantly improves conversion rates—far surpassing the industry average of 3.65% for general contractors—and drastically reduces the time and resources typically spent on initial site visits and qualifying prospects.

A Seamless Experience

User Workflows and Vetted Professionals

The HOMEase AI platform is meticulously structured to provide a seamless and secure experience for all stakeholders, featuring distinct user roles with clear, defined workflows. Homeowners begin by setting up a personal profile, then initiate an AR assessment, review the AI-generated analysis, and provide project specifics like description, budget, and urgency to create a lead. They can then view matched contractors, review proposals, select a professional, communicate via real-time chat, process secure payments through Stripe, and ultimately leave detailed ratings and reviews.

For contractors, the journey starts with a rigorous onboarding process, requiring a detailed application including company information, licenses, insurance, references, and a strong preference for Certified Aging-in-Place Specialist (CAPS) certifications. Once approved by an administrator within 3-5 business days, contractors manage their professional profiles, receive and review high-quality leads, respond with proposals, and manage projects and payments.

Administrators oversee the entire ecosystem, monitoring key platform metrics, rigorously reviewing and approving contractor applications through an integrated system, managing all user accounts, and tracking system performance. This multi-stage vetting process for contractors—including verification of state and local business licenses, proof of adequate liability and workers’ compensation insurance, and thorough background checks—is paramount to ensuring the quality and trustworthiness of the HOMEase AI network.

Strategic Growth:
Go-to-Market and the HUD OAHMP Advantage

HOMEase AI's go-to-market strategy is characterized by a phased geographic rollout combined with a powerful business-to-government (B2G) approach that strategically leverages federal funding programs.

The initial launch focuses on states with high concentrations of older adults, beginning with Texas in Phase 1 (2025-2026), followed by Florida in Phase 2 (2027-2028). A "golden opportunity" has been identified in the U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development's (HUD) Older Adults Home Modification Program (OAHMP - 14.921). This federal grant program, which awarded $109 million to 67 grantees in January 2025, provides funds for low-cost, high-impact modifications for low-income seniors.

Crucially, the program mandates that an initial in-home assessment be conducted by a licensed Occupational Therapist (OT) or a CAPS-certified professional under OT supervision. This requirement perfectly aligns with HOMEase AI's core capabilities: standardized AR assessment tools and access to a vetted network of CAPS-certified contractors. The over 67 awarded grantee organizations have an immediate, grant-mandated need for the exact services HOMEase AI provides, allowing them to efficiently deploy grant funds within the program's $5,000 per-unit cost cap.

The phased B2G plan targets Area Agencies on Aging (AAAs) and OAHMP grantees in Months 1-6, Centers for Independent Living (CILs) in Months 3-9, and later expands to CDBG Home Repair Programs and Weatherization Assistance Programs, offering scalable assessment and contractor management solutions to government-funded organizations.

// The Foundation: Robust Technical Architecture and Financial Outlook

The HOMEase AI platform is built on a modern, serverless, and event-driven architecture hosted exclusively on the Google Cloud Platform (GCP), ensuring scalability, security, and operational efficiency. Core asynchronous workflows, such as AR analysis and lead matching, are managed via Google Cloud Pub/Sub, guaranteeing a responsive user experience.

The robust technology stack includes Next.js 15 (App Router) for the frontend deployed on Google Cloud Run, Next.js API Routes and serverless Google Cloud Functions (2nd Gen) for the backend, Google Firestore (NoSQL) for the database, Firebase Authentication with NextAuth.js v5, Google Cloud Storage for files, and Stripe Connect/Checkout for payments. AI/ML capabilities are powered by Google Gemini API for analysis and Fal.ai for visualization.

Security is paramount, with a multi-layered model incorporating Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) via Firebase Custom Claims, strict input validation using Zod, a comprehensive Content Security Policy (CSP), and encryption for all data at rest and in transit. Developed under the codename "LeadMatch AI," the platform has a mature development history, with its initial full-featured launch (v1.0.0) on January 1, 2024, followed by continuous enhancements.

Financially, HOMEase AI projects robust growth and profitability, with revenue forecast to grow from $1.2 million in 2025 to $161.0 million by 2030. Net profit margins are expected to exceed 50% by 2027, demonstrating a capital-efficient and highly profitable business model. Key performance indicators for the initial phases target 10+ AAA meetings scheduled, 2-3 pilot partners signed, 25+ contractor sign-ups in pilot areas, 500+ assessments completed, and over $100K in B2G revenue within the first year.

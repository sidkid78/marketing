The Longevity Economy

And the Bell County Vision


The longevity economy is a current reality, not a distant projection. As the demographic shift toward an older population gains momentum, the demand for aging-in-place modifications has increased significantly.

However, the connection between homeowners and qualified contractors often remains inefficient. In Bell County, Texas, we are launching a pilot program designed to revolutionize this engagement.

This guide outlines the strategic framework for identifying, engaging, and onboarding high-caliber contractors. We are seeking partners who understand the specialized needs of older adults, specifically focusing on Certified Aging-in-Place Specialists (CAPS).

This ensures homeowners receive expert care while contractors benefit from a streamlined, data-driven workflow that eliminates traditional bidding frustrations. This pilot is built on the principle of mutual efficiency, utilizing AR-validated lead reports and precise site data to remove ambiguity from home modifications.

The Strategic Priority

Partnering with CAPS-Certified Professionals


The cornerstone of the Bell County pilot is the recruitment of Certified Aging-in-Place Specialists (CAPS). This designation, developed by the National Association of Home Builders (NAHB) in collaboration with AARP, signifies a professional with rigorous training in universal design and the unique requirements of the aging population.

CAPS professionals possess the technical expertise and specialized knowledge of universal design required to navigate the complexities of aging-in-place modifications. These specialists are our primary targets because they already value the social mission of fall prevention and hospital readmission reduction.

TARGET: SMALL-TO-MID-SIZED CREWS
SPECIALTIES: CURBLESS SHOWERS, ADA RAMPS, NON-SLIP FLOORING

When sourcing these partners, we prioritize small-to-mid-sized crews who seek qualified leads but are dissatisfied with generic lead-generation platforms that provide low-intent inquiries. By prioritizing CAPS-certified crews, we build immediate trust with homeowners and medical professionals.

The Value Proposition

Efficiency as a Competitive Edge


The primary friction point for any contractor is the 'wasted bid'—the hours spent traveling to a site and drafting a quote for a client who may not be ready to proceed. Our pilot eliminates this inefficiency.

We frame the pilot as an opportunity for scarcity and operational excellence. We provide contractors with AR-validated lead reports that include room-level risk flags, high-resolution photos, and suggested scopes of work. This allows the contractor to provide an accurate estimate without an initial site visit, significantly increasing their return on investment.

Furthermore, we offer 'ZIP code exclusivity' during the pilot phase, ensuring partners are not competing with numerous crews for the same lead. Early adopters are granted 'Founding Contractor' status, which includes a digital badge for marketing and locked-in early-bird pricing for future phases. By providing technical specifications—including ADA-compliant measurements—we empower contractors to quote faster and more accurately than regional competitors.

Sourcing & Outreach

Identifying High-Quality Crews


Finding the right contractors requires a targeted approach. Start with the NAHB CAPS directory, filtering for the Killeen and Temple areas. Supplement this by utilizing specific search strings such as 'CAPS certified contractor Bell County' or 'aging in place contractor Killeen Temple.'

Digital community hubs are also valuable; monitor groups like 'Central Texas Contractors' to identify professionals who maintain a professional digital presence and exhibit responsiveness.

MINIMUM CRITERIA

  • FULLY LICENSED & INSURED
  • 24-HOUR LEAD RESPONSE WINDOW
  • COLLABORATIVE FEEDBACK LOOP

This feedback is essential for refining our AR-validated reports and ensuring the platform meets the real-world needs of field professionals.

The Multi-Channel Outreach Playbook

Our outreach strategy utilizes three distinct channels to maximize engagement:

High-Intent Email

Subject lines must emphasize immediate value, such as: 'Pre-qualified home modification leads in Bell County.' The content should be concise, explaining that we are piloting a local safety assessment platform where leads include photos, measurements, and risk summaries at no cost during the pilot phase.

Direct Digital Messaging

Contractors often manage operations via mobile devices; a direct message on professional social platforms is frequently more effective than email. Use this script: 'We are piloting a local home-safety assessment application providing pre-qualified leads for Bell County homeowners. Would you like to review a sample report?'

On-Site Engagement

Visit industry supply houses during early morning hours to connect with decision-makers. Provide a one-page pilot summary and a brief pitch: 'We are providing pre-qualified safety leads for accessibility work during a local pilot program.'

Operational Standards

Success depends on maintaining high operational standards. Once a contractor expresses interest, the onboarding process must be efficient. We provide a sample lead report immediately to demonstrate data quality.

During the initial onboarding call, we reiterate the 24-hour response requirement. In the aging-in-place market, speed is a safety requirement, as delays can result in preventable accidents.

We also emphasize the professional networking benefits; by joining our ecosystem, contractors gain access to a network of local suppliers and senior care organizations. This positioning aligns their business goals with a social mission: helping seniors remain in their homes safely. We are providing a specialized niche in a rapidly growing segment of the residential remodeling industry.
